About Me

I’m Shovon Munshi, a Naval Architecture and Marine Engineering graduate from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology. I led the design of a 1000 DWT inland cargo ship, ensuring compliance with engineering standards while optimizing its performance for Bangladesh’s river routes using AutoCAD, Rhinoceros 3D, Excel, and Maxsurf under the guidance of Dr. Md. Shahidul Islam. My thesis research, supervised by Prof. Dr. Zobair ibn Awal, on inland waterway accidents, employed SARIMA, SARIMAX, and HWES models to uncover accident trends and proposed data-driven safety policies, showcasing my passion for blending data analysis with engineering impact.


As president of the BUET Origami Club, I organized multiple events and grew membership by 18% in six months, honing my leadership skills through collaboration and creativity. In addition to this, I mentor over 10 students preparing for university, fostering their aspirations while sharpening my communication skills.


I am interested in data analysis, with a particular focus on data-driven research in engineering system optimization and predictive analytics.
